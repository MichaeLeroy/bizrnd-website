What's Cooking

Scones cover a lot of territory. The recipe is for what might be considered British style scones, biscuit like, flavored, quick breads. I usually go for sweet, but one can certainly adjust the flavoring and mix-ins for a savory effect.

This recipe is derived from one that claims a yield of 8 scones, but I find my self using it for 4 large scones, each of which can be a meal in itself.

Sources

I looked through several scones recipes and tried a couple before finding one that I liked, allrecipes' Simple Scones, which served as the main inspiration for this one.

Tools and Utensils

No exotic tools are needed for this recipe.

  • Mixing Bowl (say 2 to 4 quart)
  • Dry Measuring Cups and Spoons
  • Pastry Blender (nice to have but I don't)
  • Liquid Measuring Cup
  • An Ordinary Fork
  • Pastry Mat
  • Baking Sheet
  • Oven
  • Cooling Racks

Ingredients

Dry

2 Cups Flour

I've tried a several flours. So far, I've gotten the best results with King Arthur All Purpose. This flour is said to be rather high in protein, so a good bread flour night work as well. I'd advise against pastry flour; I found that adding it to the recipe results in cake-like scones.

⅓ Cup Sugar

Or the dry sweetener of you choice.

1–½ Teaspoons Baking Powder

The source recipe calls for a mix of Baking Powder, Baking Soda and Salt. I'm a skeptic of this traditional approach to quick breads. So I omit the salt and soda, adding a bit more Baking Powder than called for in the original recipe.

Powdered Spice

Here you can exercise some creativity, choosing a spice appropriate to the sort of scone you're making. For sweet scones, I often use a touch of Penzeys Chinese Five Spice Powder, which provides a nice depth of flavor. If you're making a savory scone, consider adding pepper or a curry powder. This flavoring should be in the background rather than the foreground, so add your spices with a light touch.

Shortening

6 to 8 Tablespoons of Unsalted Butter

I like to use a good quality butter, which can add nicely to the flavor of the scones. By tweaking the amount of butter, one can adjust the richness of the scones, from dry to dripping with butter.

Mix-ins

The mix-in ingredients will define the flavor of your scones, and here is where you can be most creative. Consider: fresh fruit, dried fruit, chopped nuts, chocolate chips, candies, sun dried tomatoes, or roasted garlic. I often use a couple of mix-ins. Don't overload the scone with the mix-ins; 1 to 1–½ Cups generally works well. If in doubt, use less rather than more.

Liquids

½ Cup Milk

1 Egg

Liquid Flavor

This is an optional ingredient. Vanilla is conventional. Instead, I often add a liqueur, such as Drambuie or Cointreau. As with the dry spice, use sparingly for background flavoring.

Preparations

The night before the cook

  • throw a stick of butter in the freezer

At the outset of the cook:

  • preheat the oven to 400℉.
  • if necessary, prep the mix-ins
  • have some space available for ingredient assembly
  • have the pastry mat laid out
  • lay out the cooling racks, keeping in mind that they'll shed crumbs, moisture and possibly shortening

Cook

Thoroughly mix the dry ingredients in the mixing bowl.

Cut the cold shortening into the dry ingredients. Blend until it is incorporated as small pebbles.

Stir in the mix-ins.

Prepare the liquid ingredients. Measure out the milk, add the egg and beat moderately with a fork. Add the liquid flavor.

Pour the Liquid ingredients into the mixing bowl and fold in. Avoid over mixing; stop once the liquid is incorporated. The result should be a wet, sticky, dough.

Generously dust the pastry mat and your hands with flour. Dump the dough onto the pasty mat and form it into a flat loaf. Cut this loaf into serving sized scones, flouring the edges to keep stickiness under control.

Place the scones on the baking sheet and put them into the pre-heated oven. Baking time may vary. Start checking at about 15 minutes. Once the scones have a golden brown crust, they should be ready.

Remove the scones from the oven and place them on the cooling rack. Allow them to cool for several minutes and then enjoy.
