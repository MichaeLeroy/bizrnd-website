What's Cooking

Hummus is a tasty and nutritious bean spread. The base of a traditional hummus is a puree of chickpeas and tahini.

Sources

I can cite no specific source for this recipe. It's one that I worked out over several batches.

Tools and Utensils

  • Large Bowl (4 quarts)
  • Pressure Cooker
  • Food Processor

It is certainly possible to make hummus without a pressure cooker and food processor, but these tools make the job faster and easier than cooking with a conventional pan and manual mashing and blending.

Ingredients

1–½ Cups of Dry Garbonzo Beans

6 Ounces of Tahini

8 Ounces of Roasted Pepper

3 Tablespoons of Olive Oil

Spices

Preparations

The day before the cook

  • rinse and soak the garbonzo beans

At the outset of the cook:

  • make sure that the tahini is well mixed

Cook

Rinse the garbonzo beans and put them in the pressure cooker with 4–½ cups of water.

Cook the beans at 2 bar for 12 minutes and allow the pressure cooker to cool until the pressure is relieved (a natural release).
