What's Cooking

This is a recipe for black bean veggie burgers with chili style spicing. It's certainly a treat for vegetarians, and meat eaters tired of the same old beef burger can enjoy them as well.

Sources

This is very much my own recipe. I've developed it over probably a dozen batches, learning how to improve the results with each trial. Getting a decent flavor was not too hard but achieving a decent consistency that held together well was a challenge.

Tools and Utensils

No exotic tools are needed for this recipe. A good pressure cooker helps when cooking the beans, and a food processor saves a lot of manual chopping.

  • Pressure Cooker
  • Food Processor
  • Chef's and Paring Knives
  • Liquid Measuring Cup
  • A Large Saucepan
  • A Medium Bowl
  • A Large Bowl
  • Wax Paper
  • Plastic Storage Boxes
  • A Well Cured Cast Iron Skillet

Ingredients

2 Cups Dry Black Beans

Just about any sort of bean works. I've also had good luck with pinto and garbanzo beans, but I'd take a different approach to spicing with these alternatives.

2 Tablespoon Olive Oil

½ Bulb of Garlic

Several Chili Peppers

Take you pick, mild or hot, to adjust the spiciness of the veggie burgers.

2 Tablespoons of Adobo Seasoning

2 to 3 Medium Potatoes

1 Sweet Potato

1 Beet

2 or 3 Carrots

¼ Cup of Chopped Walnuts

1 to 2 Tablespoons of Corn Meal

Preparing the Beans

One day prior to making the veggie burgers, rinse the beans and set them up to soak overnight.

Once the beans have soaked, they're ready to be cooked. Discard the soak water and rinse the beans. If using a pressure cooker, add 6 cups of water to the beans and cook for 12 minutes at maximum pressure. Allow the beans to cool naturally (rather than using a quick release method). Once the beans have cooled drain the beans, reserving about ½ cup of the liquid for cooking the other ingredients. Shake the beans a bit in colander so that the cooked beans are rather dry.

Cooking the beans without a pressure cooker will take somewhat more time. Make sure that they're cooked until they're quit tender.

Preparing the Vegetables

Finely chop the garlic and chili peppers. I remove and discard the peppers' seeds and placenta to avoid painful fumes when I cook, but that step is up to you.

Chop the root vegetables and set them aside. As they're all going into the pan together, they can be added to a common bowl.

Add the oil, garlic and chilies to the saucepan and saute under medium heat.

When the garlic and chilies are tender, add a bit of the reserved bean cooking liquid. Stir in the adobo seasoning and mix thoroughly.

Add the chopped root vegetables and walnuts, adding bean liquid and stirring to keep the ingredients from sticking to the pan. Ideally the root vegetables should be steamed tender by this process and not end up too wet. This step may take some experience to develop a good feel for achieving the right consistency. It's not terrible if these vegetables are a bit wet, but forming the veggie burgers may be messier.

As this cooked vegetable mix is cooling, chop the beans in a food processor. Once the vegetable mix has cooled enough to handle, combine it with the chopped beans in a large bowl.

Forming the Veggie Burgers

Have the plastic storage bins at hand and cut several sheets of wax paper to comfortably fit these bins.

Take a handful of the veggie burger mix and from it into a patty. You may want to adjust the cooking of future batches based on how the mix handles. If the mix is too dry, it may be difficult to get the patty to hold together. If it is too wet, the mix may stick too readily to your hands.

Sprinkle some corn meal onto the top of the patty and lay it onto wax paper in a storage bin. Then sprinkle some corm meal on the other side. Once one has filled a layer of the container cover the patties with another sheet of wax paper. It may be possible to stack 2 or 3 layers of veggie burgers in a container, depending upon its size and the uniformity of the patties.

Freeze the veggie burgers. They'll keep better frozen and be easier to handle when they are cooked.

Cooking the Veggie Burgers

Heat the oven to 350℉. I like to cook the veggie burgers in a well cured cast iron skillet, which I preheat as well. A cookie sheet also works, though the veggie burgers may be more liable to stick to the surface of the sheet. I've not tried cooking on foil, but I suspect that that doing so would be awkward..

Cook frozen veggie burgers for about 18 minutes on the first side. Then turn them and cook for another 15 minutes.

Remove from the oven, serve on buns with your favorite condiments and enjoy.
