Problems Solved Through Analysis and Creativity

Analysis, Interrogating the Problem

All too often, problems are approached with mistaken assumptions. An approach not informed by a clear picture of the situation is unlikely to succeed and will often result in delays, frustration and needless costs. The best way to gain the understanding needed to solve a difficult problem is to ask the right questions and to apply a disciplined analysis to the apparent answers to ensure that the problem and its root causes are identified and understood.

Creativity, Transforming Insights into Solutions

Once a problem has been identified and well defined, solutions become feasible, perhaps even easy. Here creativity comes into play. Brainstorming, prototyping, modeling, collaborating with line personnel and domain experts all come into play.
